ABOUT EVOLFO

To quote the Boston Herald, “This collective of ex-Berklee misfits label their music gypsy funk. Amazing a tag that rad could be so limiting. Evolfo does pop, punk, funk, ska, and Eastern European rhythms — sometimes in the same song”. The band brings together different styles to make something new. The result is raw, danceable and boisterous; an explosion of sound residing somewhere between Gogol Bordello and Dr. John, with a slew of other more obscure worldly influences."

Since they formed in high school in 2009, the veritable army that is Evolfo has been an enigma. Their music is a celebration of life! It implores you; rise up and live, get off your high-horse, forget your pretentions, your integrity, your professionalism and just dance.

Perhaps best known for their live performances, Evolfo was discovered in the literal underground scene of Allston, MA's basement shows. There in Boston they formed their current lineup. It was here that Evolfo began to achieve some notoriety. Rising up, like many Boston bands before them, out of the basement show underworld and joining the ranks of other notable Boston funk/world acts like The Macrotones and Jesse Dee. They gained much support after their first independent release in September 2012 “The Food of Love” EP, which featured a song and video for what is perhaps the best known Evolfo track “You Light Me Up”. In December 2012 they were invited to perform in front of the entire Boston music community at the Boston Music Awards, where they were also announced winners of the “International Artist of the Year” award. Since then they have been sharing stages all over the US with acts such as Red Baraat, No BS! Brass Band, Hot 8 Brass Band, Betty Who, Moon Hooch, Counting Crows and others.

In the fall of 2013, Evolfo moved from Boston to Brooklyn, jumping head first into the ever-competitive music scene that is New York City. Since that time, they have been carving a place for themselves in many different venues, making the rounds in New York. Evolfo wrote, recorded and manufactured its second frontline release without the help of a label. The two track, 7” Vinyl entitled FHF/Carry On will be released mid-September. Evolfo has always been of the “just get in the van and tour” school of thought so you never know where you might find them. They may be down south playing stages at SXSW or out west hitting such festivals as Hardly Strictly Bluegrass. Currently the best chance of catching Evolfo is at clubs and parties in the North East. If music be the food of love, play on!

FULL BIO

XMatt Gibbs
Hailed as “One helluva a human” by Crabby Times magazine, and “a dude” by TIME IN Chatanooga, Matt is truly a guy who plays music. Starting the band Evolfo was certainly an idea he had in high school, and as everyone knows ideas often turn into things. Matt started his career in his hometown of Mill Valley, CA by tuning his guitar, and by gum after that he played it! He promptly quit his job at that place, bought a van from a dude and took a band called Evolfo out on the road forever more.

XKai Sorensen
Hailing from Blaire, NE, Trumpet boy and maestro MD Kai “Old Man” Sorensen spent years practicing his horn in the closet of “The Golden Cornhusk”, where he was stripping his way through college. When he finally emerged wielding his golden horn and signature glistening gold speedo he was turning heads everywhere he went. It was this incessant head turning that eventually brought him face to face with his current position in Evolfo.

XRafferty Swink
It is a little known fact that some nuclear reactor explosion caused by an idiot released a river of green goo that made its way to the small village of Ashland, OR in July 1991. What might have been a sticky green disaster turned into a miracle as the people affected by the radioactive waste only grew to be more beautiful and talented than everyone in California, screw those guys. Rafferty Swink emerged as the leader of these beautiful mutants, wielding 8 arms, which he used to play 1000 instruments at once. It was after the legendary incident known as “the dual of 1,000 hi fives” that he and Evolfo bandleader Matt Gibbs decided to write some songs together.
XBen Adams
Ben from San Jose, CA was just a small boy in a Viking man’s world when he first traded his long sword for a trombone. Growing up on a Viking longboat takes a permanent toll on most boys, but not Ben. He took bone lessons with the legendary Hurden Gurden Jack Flargenstoe to get him through the hard times. When he finally beat old Jack Flargenstoe in a Trombone battle (to the death) that was when he knew it was time for him to finally take to dry land and join the band.
XAngelo Spampinato
Dangelo, Dangleboobs, Dangletron 3030, Maya Dangelou, Danger Load; a drummer by any other name would smell as sweet. But not Angelo. It is known that no other man has dangled as low as the man known as the “Dangliest drummer in rock and roll”. A more recent addition to the band, Angelo left his family of mango pirates in Oakland, CA to join Evolfo on a whim, leaving a trail of broken hearts in his wake.
XRonnie Lanzilotta
Tall and tan and young and lovely, Ronnie Lanzilotta goes walking, and when he passes each one he passes goes “ahhhh”. It was hard to miss Old Ron Jon Silver as he came strutting out of his weekly gig at “The Crackling Crab Stew Pot” in South, east, west, downward Long Island. Commonly referred to as “the hot guy” in Evolfo, Ronnie left his steady gig with “The Dirty Sheep Wranglers” in order to join the band in 2011.
XJared Yee
In the early days of hip-hop there was one artist known only as the mysterious “J-Yeezy”. It is said that no one saw his face for when they looked upon the stage all they saw the blinding reflection of a light in his saxophone made of pure white-gold. Ten years after the unexplained disappearance of J-Yeezy, a saxophone player by the name of Jared Yee started hanging around Santa Barbara, CA. It is said that he blasted a saxophone solo so beautiful that he sailed across the country to the east coast on a river of lady tears. Evolfo contests that this is the second coming of J-Yeezy.
XTyler Kion
It was a stormy day in winter 1901 when Tyler Kion emerged from a giant clam in the Pacific Ocean with nothing but a saxophone to cover his nudity. Whether or not he was born that day or 1,000 years before to a pair of blonde gods is up for debate. What is certain is that when his parents in Seattle, WA took him into their home in 1987 they had no idea they were raising the man would soon become known as the “tycoon of saxophone typhoons”.
